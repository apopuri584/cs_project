Contributions of Different Pollutants to Air Pollution

This visualization shows the percentage contribution of different pollution sources to overall air pollution using a bar chart. Each bar represents a specific source, allowing for easy comparison of how much each contributes to total pollution levels.

Figure 6. Contributions of Different Pollutants to Air Pollution

Our Insights
  • Remaining sources and other combustion account for the largest shares of air pollution, indicating that multiple smaller or mixed sources collectively have a major impact.
  • Solvents, commercial activities, and anthropogenic dust also contribute notable portions, highlighting the role of human-driven activities beyond major industries.
  • Transportation, industry, and agriculture contribute moderate but meaningful amounts, showing that pollution comes from a wide range of sectors.
Data Source

State of Global Air — Average Annual Population-Weighted PM₂.₅ Concentrations

Discussion

The data used for this visualization includes AQI values and industrial toxin concentrations collected across U.S. states. AQI represents overall air pollution levels, while industrial toxins measure pollution output from industrial activity. The key variables analyzed were AQI and industrial toxin concentration. Box plots were used to compare the distributions of these two metrics, making it easy to identify medians, variability, and outliers. This visualization is effective because it highlights differences in spread and extreme values, helping show how industrial pollution can vary widely even when general air quality levels appear more consistent across states.
