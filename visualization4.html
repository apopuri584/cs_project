Average Annual Population-Weighted PM₂.₅ Line Graph

This visualization shows changes in average annual population-weighted PM₂.₅ concentrations over time. Each point represents a yearly average, highlighting long-term trends and fluctuations in fine particulate air pollution.

Our Insights
  • Average PM₂.₅ levels show a clear downward trend over the time period, indicating overall improvements in air quality.
  • The most significant declines occur in the early-to-mid 2000s, suggesting the impact of stronger air quality regulations and pollution control measures.
  • Recent values are substantially lower than those in the 1990s, reflecting long-term reductions in population exposure to fine particulate matter.

Discussion

The data used for this visualization consists of annual population-weighted PM₂.₅ concentrations measured over multiple decades. PM₂.₅ refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or smaller, which poses serious health risks due to its ability to penetrate deep into the lungs. The key variables used in this analysis were year and average annual population-weighted PM₂.₅ concentration. A line graph was used to display how air pollution levels have changed over time, making it easy to observe long-term trends, periods of rapid improvement, and years with slight increases. This visualization is effective because it emphasizes overall progress in air quality while still showing year-to-year variability, helping illustrate how environmental policies and external factors influence pollution exposure over time.
