Distribution of Air Quality Metrics Across U.S. States

This visualization compares the distribution of Air Quality Index (AQI) values and industrial toxin levels across U.S. states using box plots. Each box plot shows the spread, median, and outliers for each metric, highlighting variability in air quality and pollution levels.

Figure 5. Distribution of Air Quality Metrics Across U.S. States

Our Insights
  • AQI values are more tightly clustered, suggesting less variation in overall air quality across states.
  • Industrial toxin levels show a much wider spread, indicating significant differences in industrial pollution between states.
  • Several high outliers for industrial toxins suggest localized areas with unusually high pollution concentrations.
Data Source

Air Quality by State — World Population Review

Discussion

The data used for this visualization includes AQI values and industrial toxin concentrations collected across U.S. states. AQI represents overall air pollution levels, while industrial toxins measure pollution output from industrial activity. The key variables analyzed were AQI and industrial toxin concentration. Box plots were used to compare the distributions of these two metrics, making it easy to identify medians, variability, and outliers. This visualization is effective because it highlights differences in spread and extreme values, helping show how industrial pollution can vary widely even when general air quality levels appear more consistent across states.
