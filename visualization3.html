AQI by State: 2020 vs 2025

This grouped bar chart compares the Air Quality Index (AQI) for each U.S. state between 2020 and 2025. For each state, two bars are shown side by side, allowing for direct comparison of air quality conditions before and after the COVID-19 pandemic period. AQI values from 2020 are shown in brown, while 2025 AQI values are shown in blue. This visualization highlights how air quality levels have changed over time across states and makes trends, increases, or decreases in AQI easy to observe.

Figure 3. AQI by State: 2020 vs 2025

Our Insights
  • Most states show higher AQI values in 2025 compared to 2020, indicating a general decline in air quality over time.
  • The magnitude of change varies by state, suggesting that local factors such as population growth, industrial activity, and transportation patterns influence air quality differently across regions.
  • States with relatively lower AQI in 2020 often still experience noticeable increases by 2025, highlighting that air quality challenges are widespread rather than isolated.

Discussion

We chose to focus on U.S. air quality trends because understanding changes in pollution levels over time can help highlight environmental challenges and inform public health decisions. A grouped bar chart was selected for this visualization because it allows for a clear comparison of AQI values between 2020 and 2025 for each state, making trends, increases, or decreases easy to observe at a glance. The primary data source was the NIH HDPulse Air Pollution dataset, which provides average AQI values for each state. This data was combined with U.S. state geographic information to ensure accurate representation by location. In the grouped bar chart, the x-axis represents individual states, while the y-axis shows the corresponding AQI values, with two bars per state (brown for 2020 and blue for 2025) to illustrate changes over time.
