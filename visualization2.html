Industrial Waste vs Air Quality Index Scatter Plot

This visualization shows the relationship between Air Quality Index (AQI) values and industrial toxic concentration. Each point represents an observation, highlighting trends and variability in air quality.

Figure 2. Industrial Waste vs Air Quality Index Scatter Plot

Our Insights
  • Higher AQI values are generally associated with higher levels of industrial toxic concentration, indicating a positive relationship between industrial activity and air pollution.
  • The spread of data points suggests that industrial emissions are not the only factor influencing AQI, as similar air quality levels can correspond to different pollution concentrations.
  • Outliers with unusually high toxic concentration levels suggest localized industrial impacts that may significantly affect air quality.
Data Source

Air Quality by State — World Population Review

Discussion

The data used for this visualization includes air quality measurements and industrial waste data. The AQI dataset provides numerical values representing air pollution levels, while the industrial waste dataset records the amount of industrial waste measured in parts per million (ppm). The key columns used in this analysis were AQI for air quality and industrial waste (ppm) for pollution output, allowing for a direct comparison between the two variables. Using these datasets, a scatter plot was created to examine the relationship between industrial waste and air quality. Each point on the plot represents a data observation, and a best-fit trend line was added to emphasize the overall correlation between AQI and industrial waste levels. This visualization is effective because it reveals both the general trend and the variability in the data, making it easier to identify patterns, correlations, and outliers that may not be apparent from summary statistics alone.
